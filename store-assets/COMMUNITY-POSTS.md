# Community launch posts (value first, brand aligned)

Rules of thumb before posting:
- Read each community's self promotion rules first. Many allow it on certain
  days or in certain threads only.
- Lead with the problem and the help, not the download. Be a person, not an ad.
- Always disclose it is your app. Honesty builds trust and avoids removals.
- No em dashes. Nothing that reads as machine written.
- Links: Play opt in https://play.google.com/apps/testing/granularvolume.com
  Source https://github.com/Rzuss/granular-volume
  Demo https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8dR0nNhW27g

---

## 1. Sensitive hearing (r/hyperacusis, r/misophonia, r/tinnitus)

Tone: empathetic, personal, never salesy. These communities dislike promotion,
so frame it as something you made to solve a real problem and are sharing.

Title: I got tired of my phone's lowest volume still being too loud, so I made a free app for it

Body:
For a while the lowest volume step on my phone was still too loud for quiet
listening, especially with headphones late at night. Android does not let you
go below that step, so I built a small free app that adds finer volume levels
underneath it. It puts a little dial on screen that you can drag anywhere, and
it lowers the output well below the usual minimum.

It is free, has no ads, collects nothing, and is open source. I am not selling
anything. If quiet listening is hard for you too, it might help. Happy to answer
questions or take suggestions on what would make it more comfortable to use.

## 2. Audiophiles (r/headphones, r/headphoneadvice, r/iems)

Tone: technical, peer to peer. They value non invasive and open source.

Title: Free, open source app that adds volume steps below Android's minimum, for sensitive IEMs

Body:
If you run sensitive IEMs or anything where step one is already too loud, you
know Android's volume granularity runs out exactly where you need it most. I
built a small app that adds fine attenuation below the lowest hardware step,
applied on the output, with a floating dial you can place anywhere.

It does not override your volume buttons or replace the system volume panel, so
it stays out of the way and does not fight with your music. No EQ, no presets,
just the quiet end of the dial. Free, no ads, no tracking, GPL-3.0. Source and a
short demo in the comments. Feedback welcome.

## 3. Parents (r/NewParents, r/beyondthebump, local baby groups)

Tone: warm, practical.

Title: Free app to keep white noise and lullabies barely audible for a sleeping baby

Body:
We use white noise and quiet lullabies at night, and the phone's lowest volume
was still louder than I wanted right next to the crib. I made a small free app

## 4. Android apps (r/androidapps, r/fossandroid)

Title: Granular Volume, a free and open source app for volume below Android's minimum

Body:
Sharing a small app I built. Android's lowest volume step is often still too
loud for quiet rooms or sensitive headphones, and the system will not go lower.
Granular Volume adds fine steps below that minimum through a floating dial you
can place anywhere and close with one tap.

It is single purpose by design, no EQ maze and no account. It does not override
your volume buttons or the system volume UI, it just sits on top. Free, no ads,
no tracking, GPL-3.0. Source and demo linked. Happy to take feedback.
