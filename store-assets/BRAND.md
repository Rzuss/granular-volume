# Granular Volume: Brand & Positioning

> The strategy doc for how Granular Volume is positioned, described, and marketed.
> Primary angle: functional led, comfort layered underneath.

## The one line

**Granular Volume is the quiet dial for Android. It adds the volume levels your phone leaves out, below the lowest setting, in a floating control that never touches your system volume.**

## Hero tagline

**Quieter than your phone allows.**

Secondary line for store search and headers:
**When the lowest setting is still too loud.**

## The gap we own

Almost every volume app on the store goes one direction: louder. Boosters, amplifiers, gain. The opposite direction, quieter than the hardware minimum, is nearly empty, yet people actively search for it (forum threads, an F-Droid feature request, blog write ups all asking how to lower Android below its minimum). That uncontested space is the brand.

## Why we win

Most volume apps in this category are maximalist audio suites (EQ, hundreds of steps, presets, automation) that override the system volume UI and the volume buttons, which can behave inconsistently across devices. Our wedge:

| | Typical volume app | Granular Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Philosophy | Swiss army knife for audio | One thing, done perfectly: quiet below minimum |
| Invasiveness | Overrides volume buttons and system UI | Touches nothing, sits on top as an overlay |
| Stability | Override and automation can break per device | Stable because it never intercepts the system |
| Price and trust | Paid | Free, no ads, no tracking, open source (GPL-3.0) |
| FOSS reach | Closed | Eligible for F-Droid, a whole channel they cannot serve |

One sentence: **Every other volume app makes things louder, or rebuilds your whole audio system. We do the one thing none of them focus on: make it quieter than minimum, without touching anything.**

## Brand pillars

1. **Direction: the quiet, not the loud.** We own the underserved direction.
2. **Non invasive.** No button override, no system UI takeover. Exactly what breaks elsewhere.
3. **Single purpose simplicity.** One floating dial. No EQ maze, no account.
4. **Trust.** Free, private, open source.

## Audiences (functional hook, emotional payoff)

- **Night listeners and parents.** White noise for a baby, a podcast to fall asleep to, a partner asleep beside them. They need barely audible.
- **Sensitive hearing.** Hyperacusis, misophonia, tinnitus, sensory processing, migraine. Vocal, loyal, high word of mouth.
- **Audiophiles with sensitive gear.** IEMs, bone conduction, high sensitivity headphones where step one is already too loud. They value non invasive and open source.
- **Quiet environments.** Library, open office, lab, meditation, ASMR.

## Voice and tone

Calm, precise, quietly confident. Short sentences. We describe a feeling of control and relief, not hype. We never shout (it would contradict the product). No exclamation stacking, no growth hacking tone.

## Copy rules

No em dashes or en dashes in any public copy. Nothing that reads as machine generated. Plain, human, specific.

## Visual identity

Nocturnal and low light, matching the quiet and night use. Deep indigo to violet gradient (`#16162B` to `#4A43C9`) with a soft cyan accent (`#22d3ee`). The icon motif is descending volume bars, a dial winding down toward calm. Keep the existing icon, extend the system into the feature graphic and screenshots with the same dark, restful palette.

## Go to market

1. **ASO first.** Title and description target the real search intent (quieter, lower volume, minimum too loud, fine volume).
2. **F-Droid.** GPL-3.0, no tracking, no ads, fully eligible. Free distribution plus the FOSS audience.
3. **Communities, value first.** Share the solution where the pain lives (sleep, hyperacusis, headphones, parenting), as a helpful answer, not an ad.
4. **Content.** Short demo clips per use case (baby asleep, IEM at 2 percent, library), reusing the promo pipeline.
