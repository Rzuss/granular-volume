Why the lowest step is still too loud

Android divides the range between silence and full output into a fixed number of steps. The lowest step above mute is a fixed fraction of the maximum, not a fixed loudness. Phones and tablets are tuned for a loud maximum, so that first fraction can be well above what sensitive ears want, and it is worse through in-ear headphones that sit directly in the ear canal. Stock Android has no setting to place a level below that step. That gap is the entire problem.

What doesn't solve it

Apps that add more volume steps, like Sound Assistant's 1% increments or custom step-count apps, only subdivide the range you already have. If the lowest step was too loud, a finer lowest step is still the same loudness. Lowering the equalizer or disabling audio enhancements helps a little on some devices as a side effect, but not reliably. None of these place a level below the floor.

What does: attenuation below the floor

The one method that genuinely gets quieter applies negative gain to Android's media output through its own DynamicsProcessing framework. Because it attenuates the audio itself rather than working inside the existing steps, it produces output below the hardware minimum. It only reduces level and never boosts, so there is no distortion or added loudness in that direction. It works with any headphones, wired or wireless, and on phones and tablets alike.

Granular Volume works this way: a small floating dial with seven steps, from no change down to 30 dB below your hardware minimum. You pick the level that is comfortable for you, keep a masking sound barely there, or turn media down further than the slider allows, without touching your volume buttons or the system panel.

FAQ

Why is even the lowest volume too loud for my sensitive ears?
The lowest step above mute is a fixed fraction of the maximum, not a fixed loudness. On a device tuned for a loud maximum, that first step can be well above comfortable, and Android has no setting below it.
Is this a treatment for tinnitus or a hearing-protection device?
No. It is a convenience utility to set media quieter than your phone's minimum. It is not a medical device, not hearing protection, and not a treatment for any condition. For medical concerns, consult a qualified professional.
How does it get quieter than the minimum setting?
It applies negative gain to Android's media output using the DynamicsProcessing framework, producing output below the lowest hardware step. It only reduces level; it never boosts past the maximum.
Does it add hiss or change the sound?
No. It is a clean gain-reduction stage, not an equalizer or compressor, so it does not reshape the signal. It is the same audio, turned down further than the slider can go.
Can I use it for quiet masking sounds at night?
Yes. Many people keep white noise or soft audio barely audible when the lowest normal step is too loud. You choose the level; use your own judgment on what is comfortable.
Does it work with sensitive IEMs and low-impedance headphones?
Yes. Those are exactly the case where the first step is often already too loud. The attenuation is applied to the output, so it works with any headphones.

Yes. Free, no ads, no account, no in-app purchases, and no internet permission at all. Open source under GPL-3.0.
